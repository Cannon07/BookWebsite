Suppose we have a system of $n$ qubits along with a parameterized
Hamiltonian of the form

$$\begin\{aligned\}
    H &= H(\vec\{\lambda\})
\end\{aligned\}$$ where $$\begin\{aligned\}
\vec\{\lambda\} &= (\lambda_1, \dotsc, \lambda_q)
\end\{aligned\}$$ are the parameters. These are subsampled during the
meta-training process.

The meta-variational quantum eigensolver[@cervera2020meta] aims to find
the ground state energy of a parameterized Hamiltonian. The circuit is
initialized as $$\begin\{aligned\}
    |0\rangle^\{\otimes n\}
\end\{aligned\}$$ The circuit itself is factorized into unitary
$\mathcal\{S\}$ which depends on $\lambda_i$ and $\Phi$. This is followed
by unitary $\mathcal\{U\}$ that depends on $\Theta$.
