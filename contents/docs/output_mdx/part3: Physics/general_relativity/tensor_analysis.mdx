A tensor of type $$\begin\{aligned\}
\big ( \begin\{smallmatrix\} 0 \\ N \end\{smallmatrix\} \big )
\end\{aligned\}$$ is a function that maps a collection of $N$ vectors into
a real number. This function must be linear in each argument. For
example, the inner product $\cdot$ is a
$\big(\begin\{smallmatrix\}0 \\ 2 \end\{smallmatrix\} \big)$ tensor since

$$\begin\{aligned\}
(\alpha \vec\{v\}) \cdot \vec\{w\} &= \alpha (\vec\{v\} \cdot \vec\{w\}) \\
(\vec\{v\} + \vec\{w\}) \cdot \vec\{u\} &= \vec\{v\} \cdot \vec\{u\} + \vec\{w\} \cdot \vec\{u\} 
\end\{aligned\}$$ (Since the inner product is symmetric, the same
properties hold true in the other argument). The *metric* tensor is
defined to be the tensor corresponding to the inner product
$$\begin\{aligned\}
g(\vec\{v\}, \vec\{w\}) = \vec\{v\} \cdot \vec\{w\}
\end\{aligned\}$$

As another example, if $f$ is a function, then its total derivative $df$
is a $\big(\begin\{smallmatrix\}0 \\ 1 \end\{smallmatrix\}\big)$ tensor
(also called a one-form). More generally, a one form is a function from
vectors to the real numbers.

Note that one-forms form a vector space since functions can be added or
multiplied by a scalar. We can also talk about \"components\" of the
one-form as where they map basis vectors $$\begin\{aligned\}
p_\{\alpha\} &= \tilde\{p\}(\vec\{e\}_\{\alpha\})
\end\{aligned\}$$

The metric tensor provides a ready collection of one-forms defined by
inner products against fixed vectors $$\begin\{aligned\}
g(\cdot, \vec\{w\}) &= \cdot \vec\{w\}
\end\{aligned\}$$

A $\big(\begin\{smallmatrix\}M \\ 0 \end\{smallmatrix\}\big)$ tensor is a
linear function of $M$ one-forms to the real numbers.
