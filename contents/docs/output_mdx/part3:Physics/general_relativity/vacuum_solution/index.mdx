In this chapter, we study the vacuum solution to the Einstein field
equations. This is the case in which no gravitational fields are
present. The only spherically symmetric vacuum solution is called the
Schwarzschild metric

## Mathematical Formulation

The vacuum Einstein field equation is given by $$\begin\{aligned\}
    G_\{\mu \nu\} &= 0\\
    R_\{\mu \nu\} - \frac\{1\}\{2\}g_\{\mu \nu\}R &= 0
\end\{aligned\}$$ The Schwarzschild metric is defined by $$\begin\{aligned\}
    ds^2 *= -(1 - \frac\{2GM\}\{r\})dt^2 + (1 - \frac\{2GM\}\{r\})^\{-1\} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2
\end\{aligned\}$$ Here we have $d\Omega^2$ is the metric on the sphere
$$\begin\{aligned\}
    d\Omega^2 &= d\theta^2 + \sin^2 \theta d\phi^2
\end\{aligned\}$$ We can use this metric to solve for the Christoffel
symbols.

$$\begin\{aligned\}
\Gamma^t_\{tr\} &= \partial_r \alpha \\
\Gamma^\theta_\{r\theta\} &= \frac\{1\}\{r\} \\
\Gamma^r_\{\phi\phi\} &= -re^\{-2\beta\}\sin^2 \theta \\
\Gamma^r_\{tt\} &= e^\{2(\alpha - \beta)\} \partial_r \alpha \\
\Gamma^r_\{\theta\theta\} &= -re^\{-2\beta\} \\
\Gamma^\theta_\{\phi \phi\} &= -\sin \theta \cos \theta \\
\Gamma^r_\{rr\} &= \partial_r \beta \\
\Gamma^\phi_\{r\phi\} &= \frac\{1\}\{r\} \\
\Gamma^\phi_\{\theta \phi\} &= \frac\{\cos \theta\}\{\sin \theta\}
\end\{aligned\}$$
